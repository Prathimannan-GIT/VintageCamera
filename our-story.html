Our Story

A passion for preserving photographic history and sharing the art of vintage photography

Our Journey

2010 - The Beginning

Founded by photography enthusiasts in a small workshop, our store began as a passion project to restore and preserve vintage cameras. We started with just a handful of cameras and a dream to share the beauty of analog photography with others.

2013 - Growing Community

As our reputation grew, so did our community. We expanded our workshop and began offering repair services, helping photographers keep their vintage equipment in perfect working condition. Our first website launched, reaching collectors worldwide.

2016 - Expert Recognition

Recognized as experts in vintage camera restoration, we became trusted advisors for collectors and museums. Our team of technicians mastered the art of repairing everything from early 20th century box cameras to sophisticated SLRs.

2020 - Digital Expansion

Embracing the digital age while maintaining our analog roots, we launched our comprehensive online platform. This allowed us to reach a global audience and share our passion for vintage photography with enthusiasts worldwide.

Today - Photography Heritage

Now a leading authority in vintage cameras, we continue to preserve photographic history while educating the next generation of photographers. Our store stands as a testament to the enduring beauty and craftsmanship of analog photography.

Mission & Vision

Our Mission

To preserve and celebrate the art of vintage photography by providing authentic equipment, expert restoration services, and educational resources that inspire photographers to discover the timeless beauty of analog imaging.

Our Vision

To be the world's most trusted destination for vintage camera enthusiasts, where photographic heritage is preserved, knowledge is shared, and the magic of analog photography continues to inspire future generations.

Our Values

Authenticity, craftsmanship, education, and community. We believe in the enduring value of mechanical precision, the artistry of optical design, and the human connection that vintage photography fosters.
